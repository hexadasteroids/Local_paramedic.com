Insect bites

symptoms:
  • Red, swollen lump developed on the skin, it may be painful or itchy
  • Breathing difficulties, dizzenes, swollen face or mouth
How to deal:
  • Remove the tick or sting if it is still in the skin
  • Wash the bited area with soap and water
  • Apply cold compress or ice pack to the swelling parts for at least 10 minutes
  • Avoid touching the swelling area or scratiching it to prevent infecting any other parts
How to remove the tick:
  • Use a pair of tweezers or a tick removal tool
  • Grib the tick as close to the skin as you can without crushing it
  • Wash the skin with water and soup several times, then apply antiseptic cream to it
When to get medical treatment:
  • Wheezing or difficulty breathing
  • Swollen face, mouth or throat
  • Fast heart rate
  • Dizzenes
  • Scorpion sting
  • Swallowing difficulties
Myths:
  • Using home remedies, such as vinegar and bicarbonate of soda, as they won't help

Burns

There are a lot of types and shapes of burns,the treatment of burns depends on the location and severity of damage

1st degree burns affect the outer layer of skin.They cause pain,redness and swelling.
How to treat 1st degree burns:
  • Hold the burned area under cool running water for 10-15 minutes,or until the pain eases,you could also apply a clean towel dampened with cool tap water
  • Remove rings or other light items from the burned area quickly,before the area swells
  • Don't break small blisters if they do break, gently
    clean the area with an antibiotic ointment,and
    cover it with a nonstick bandage,if a rash develops,
    stop using the ointment and seek medical care

    they may appear wet and shiney . If the person has any of
    these symptoms he must see a doctor or seek for a

Nose bleeding

Bleeding from the nose is a common complaint. It's rarely life-threatening, but may cause significant concern. Most nosebleeding are self-limiting , but some can recurrent.
Pathophysiology: Bleeding Typically occurs when the mucosa is eroded and vessels become exposed and Subsequently break
How to deal: The following precautions Should be imparted to the Patient :
  • Use nasal Saline Spray
  • Sneeze with mouth open
  • Do not use nasal digital manipulation
  • Avoid hot and Spicy food
  • Avoid aspirin
The following Simple instructions for self-treatment for nose bleeding should be provided:
  • Apply fim digital pressure for 5-10 minutes
  • Use an ice pack
  • Practice deep, relaxed breathing
Beware of these Common mistakes that may have dire consequences:
  • Returning the head back : This movement may result in swallowing blood wich not only leads to a feeling of need to vomit, but also make it difficult to estimate the amount of bleeding
  • Sleeping: stretching the body during nosebleeds may lead to blood reaching the breathing Path which may have dangerous consequences
  • Blocking the nose : putting tissues or cottons as a way to prevent blood from bleeds which leads to its imprisonment and increases its bleeding once the cotton is removed
  • Cleaning the nose Vigorously after the bleeding Stops : The Strong movement of the nose during the first hours after the bleeding stops increases the risk of it happening again,so it is better to be careful and deal very gently with the nose until 12 hours have passed after the bleeding stops
Causes: The causes of the nose bleeding can be divided into local causes (eg, trauma, mucosal irritation, septal abnormality, inflammatory diseases ,tumors), systemic causes (eg, blood dyscrasias, artenosderosis, hereditary hemorrhagic telangiectasia) and idiopathic causes. Local trauma is the most common cause, followed by facial trauma , foreign bodies, nasal or Sinus infections and prolonged inhalation of dry air. AIso, There is a relationship between hypertension and nosebleeding which is often misunderstood. Patient with nosebleeding commonly present with an elevated blood pressure . Nosebleeding is more Common in hypertensive patients, perhaps owing to vascular fragility from long-Standing disease.

Sun stroke

Sun stroke can happen due to the long exposure To the sun especially from 11 am to 3 pm and in which the body's heat regulating system fails.
Symptoms:
  • High body temperature
  • Altered mental state or behavior
  • AIteration in Sweating
  • Nasal & vomiting
  • Flushed skin
  • Rapid breathing
  • Racing heart rate
  • Headache
Treatment:
    Take immediate action to cool the overheated person while waiting for emergency
  • Get he person into Shade or indoors
  • Remove excess clothing
  • Cool the Peperson with whatever means available :
    • Put the person in a cool bath of water or a cool shower
    • Spray with water
    • use sponge with Cool water
    • Fan while misting with cool water
    • Place ice packs or cold wet towels on the person's head ,neck, armpits and groin

Heart attacks

The Symptoms of heart attacks are life-threatening emergency that require quick action. Don't ignore even minor heart attack Symptoms.
The major Symptoms of a heart attacks are:
  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Feeling weak, light-headed, or faint
  • Pain or discomfort in the jaw, neck, or back
  • Pain or discomfort in one or both arms and shoulders
  • Shortness of breath
Other symptoms of a heart attack could include unsual or unexplained tiredness and nusa or vomiting. Women are more ikely to have these other symptoms.
